# SA Canon — the physical-reality anchor

> Distilled from Leopold Aschenbrenner, *Situational Awareness: The Decade Ahead*
> (June 2024, situational-awareness.ai). This is the canonical reference the
> **Physical World** and **Human Development** departments anchor their reasoning
> to. It is a summary of a public essay — facts must still be verified live, not
> recited from here.

---

## 1. The one idea

**AI scaling is a massive *industrial* process, not a software one.** Each new
model needs a giant new cluster, soon giant new power plants, eventually giant
new fabs. So the binding limits are *physical*: electrons, transformers,
packaging, memory — things money alone cannot instantly buy.

> "AI is a massive industrial process: each new model requires a giant new
> cluster, soon giant new power plants, and eventually giant new chip fabs." (IIIa)

---

## 2. The physical bottleneck ladder (anchor for `physical`)

Ranked by how binding the constraint is:

1. **Power / electricity — THE single biggest constraint.**
   - "Probably the single biggest constraint on the supply-side will be power." (p83)
   - Power contracts are long-term locked; a new gigawatt-class nuclear plant takes ~a decade.
   - Real near-term path = natural gas (Marcellus/Utica shale); ~$100B capex for 100GW.
   - Watch: **voltage transformers, gas turbines, PPA prices, grid interconnect queues.**
2. **Advanced packaging (CoWoS) + HBM memory — *already* key bottlenecks** (p86), tighter than raw logic fabs.
3. **Leading-edge logic (TSMC)** — needs to expand ~2x faster than its recent pace.
4. Cooling, datacenter construction, permitting, land.

**Cluster power ladder (situational-awareness reference class):**

| Year | Effective compute | Power | Reference |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2022 | ~GPT-4 cluster | ~10 MW | 10k homes |
| 2024 | +1 OOM | ~100 MW | 100k homes |
| 2026 | +2 OOMs (~1GW) | ~1 GW | Hoover Dam / large nuclear |
| 2028 | +3 OOMs | ~10 GW | small/medium US state |
| 2030 | +4 OOMs (trillion-$) | **~100 GW (>20% of US electricity)** | hundreds of power plants |

> Use this ladder to place a company: *what layer does its product feed, and how
> close is that layer to the binding constraint above it?*

---

## 3. The human-development arc (anchor for `human_dev`)

**"Counting the OOMs"** — effective compute compounds from three sources:

- compute scale-up: **~0.5 OOMs/year**
- algorithmic efficiency: **~0.5 OOMs/year**
- **"unhobbling"**: chatbot → agent (the hardest to quantify, often the biggest)
- (1 OOM = 10×; by end-2027 ≈ **+5 OOMs** of effective compute over GPT-4)

**The capability arc:**

| Model | ~Year | Level |
|---|---|---|
| GPT-2 | 2019 | preschooler |
| GPT-4 | 2023 | smart high-schooler |
| **AGI** | **~2027** | **AI researcher / engineer** ("strikingly plausible") |
| Superintelligence | end of decade | the intelligence explosion |

> **Intelligence explosion**: hundreds of millions of AGIs could automate AI
> research, compressing a decade of algorithmic progress (5+ OOMs) into ~1 year.

**How `human_dev` uses this:** place the asset's *demand* on this arc. Is the
demand it serves early (pre-consensus, OOMs of runway left) or mature/priced?
The most valuable window is **physically tight + on the steep part of the arc +
consensus not yet corrected** — "situational awareness" = conviction from the
trendlines *years before* the market prices it.

---

## 4. Economics anchors (support for `economics`)

- Willingness-to-spend is **not** the binding constraint today — *finding the
  physical infrastructure is* ("Where do I find 10GW?").
- Trillions of capex are justified: a drop-in remote AI worker automating a
  fraction of white-collar wages pays for the trillion-dollar cluster.
- Demand often **exceeds supply** even as capex grows 79% YoY (MSFT example).

---

## 5. How to use this canon

- `physical`: rank the asset against the **bottleneck ladder** (§2); is it at/near a binding link?
- `human_dev`: place the asset's demand on the **OOM arc** (§3); early or late?
- `economics`: use §4 for the scarcity-economics framing, then judge **is it already priced in**.
- Always **verify live** — this canon is the lens, not the latest fact.
